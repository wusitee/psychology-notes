* steps for development
Humans: develop language skills in a predictable series of stages that are universal across culture.
1. [[id:d2aba58a-295d-43ab-b56c-f5a189caf1aa][Cooing Stage]]
2. [[id:1f149efd-93e1-472b-bfac-fc11f70992c1][Babbling Stage]]
3. [[id:6cc54b94-b750-401d-b21b-5fcd7fc36b13][One-Ward Stage]]
4. [[id:df472078-3839-45fc-8aa7-e5efae3636fb][Two-Word Stage]]
5. [[id:4bb7364b-12a9-4769-8c41-e48c94c36c55][Full sentences]]
* Cooing Stage
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       d2aba58a-295d-43ab-b56c-f5a189caf1aa
:END:
Early stage of language development where infants produce repetitive, soft vowel sounds like "oo" and "ah", usually starting around two months of age.
* Babbling Stage

+ 10 months : familiarize themselves with the sounds of their native language, perception of only native [[id:aa149c8f-e5c6-48c9-8fa0-2b0de7e935e4][phoneme]].
* One-Ward Stage
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       6cc54b94-b750-401d-b21b-5fcd7fc36b13
:ROAM_ALIASES: Holophrase
:END:
Age one, child speaks mostly in single words
+ Whole idea can be expressed in one word "Go!", "Car", "kitty"
+ Child learned that sounds carry meanings.

Age two, a child speaks mostly two-or-three word statements "Get Ball".
